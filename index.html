Case Study 01
Northeastern University
Report Automation · Access + SQL
Turning a 3-day reporting marathon into a 5-minute automated process
The Senior VP of Development's quarterly reports required fundraisers to manually compile data across multiple Excel spreadsheets — a process that consumed several days of staff time every quarter. I analyzed the full workflow, designed an Access database that automatically imported raw data from source systems, applied all business logic, and exported completed reports in a structured format. What had taken days now ran in under 5 minutes, with no manual intervention required.
97%
Reduction in time to generate quarterly reports
Case Study 02
Northeastern University
Data Modeling · Snowflake
Building a single source of truth that eliminated a 400-request backlog
Following a CRM migration, the analytics team faced a backlog of 400+ data requests — each requiring manual joins across multiple views. Rather than chipping away at them one by one, I designed and built a production Snowflake data model: identifying every object the team would ever need, joining the relevant views, creating standardized custom fields, and promoting the result as a shared production schema. Every analyst on the team could now report from one trusted, consistent source. The backlog reached zero.
400
Backlogged requests resolved through single infrastructure build
Case Study 03
Northeastern University
Statistical Modeling · R
Segmenting 3,000 prospects so the research director knew exactly where to start
The Director of Prospect Research had 3,000 previously vetted donor prospects that needed re-evaluation — but no bandwidth to re-vet them all. I built a clustering model in R using giving history, engagement patterns, and constituent attributes to segment all 3,000 prospects into four prioritized tiers (A through D). The A-tier prospects represented the highest-value targets based on model signals. The director could now focus vetting time where it mattered most, rather than working through the full list arbitrarily.
3K
Prospects segmented and prioritized using R clustering model
Case Study 04
Northeastern University
Predictive Modeling · R · Production Deployment
Building a "Next Best Prospect" model whose scores went into production
The Prospect Development team needed a smarter way to identify which constituents were most likely to become viable major gift prospects — but with thousands of records and limited vetting bandwidth, manual review wasn't scalable. Working alongside a fellow senior analyst, I developed a suite of analytical scores using R that evaluated constituents across multiple dimensions of giving likelihood. What made this project exceptional was the outcome: the scores didn't just inform a one-time report — they went into production, becoming a permanent part of the prospect development infrastructure that the team relied on going forward. This is my favorite project of my career.
∞
Scores deployed to production — used continuously by the prospect development team
Case Study 05
Northeastern University
Tableau · Global Data · Multi-Region Dashboards
A global constituent intelligence suite across 9 international regions
What began as a single dashboard request for Southeast Asia constituents evolved into one of the most expansive visualization projects of my career. The Global Engagement team needed a holistic view of their international constituents — who they were, where they were, and how they were connected to the university. I designed and built a full dashboard series for each of 9 regions: Asia, South Asia, Southeast Asia, Latin America, the Pacific Oceanic area, and more. Each series gave the team consistent, region-specific visibility into their constituent base — enabling smarter outreach, event planning, and engagement strategy across three continents.
